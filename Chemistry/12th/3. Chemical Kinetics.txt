Introduction to Chemical Kinetics

- Chemical kinetics: The study of rates of chemical reactions and factors affecting them
- Focuses on:
  1. How fast reactions occur
  2. The mechanism of reactions
  3. Factors influencing reaction rates

Key Concepts:
- Change in concentration of reactants/products over time
- Reaction mechanisms and intermediates
- Effect of temperature, concentration, and catalysts on reaction rates

3.2 Factors Influencing Rate of a Reaction

3.2.1 Dependence of Rate on Concentration

- Rate law: Mathematical expression showing the relationship between rate and concentration
- General form: Rate = k[A]À£[B] ∏
  - k = rate constant
  - x, y = orders with respect to reactants A and B

3.2.2 Rate Expression and Rate Constant

- Rate constant (k): Proportionality factor in the rate law
- Units depend on the overall order of reaction
- Differential rate equation: -d[R]/dt = k[A]À£[B] ∏

3.2.3 Order of a Reaction

Definition: Sum of powers of concentration terms in the rate law

- Overall order = x + y (from rate law: Rate = k[A]À£[B] ∏)
- Can be zero, fractional, or integer
- Determined experimentally, cannot be predicted from balanced equation

3.2.4 Molecularity of a Reaction

Definition: Number of reacting species (atoms, ions, or molecules) that collide simultaneously in an elementary reaction

- Only applicable to elementary reactions
- Always a whole number (1, 2, or 3)
- Types:
  1. Unimolecular: One reacting species
  2. Bimolecular: Two reacting species
  3. Termolecular: Three reacting species (rare)

Key Differences between Order and Molecularity:
1. Order is experimental, molecularity is theoretical
2. Order can be fractional, molecularity is always whole number
3. Order applies to both elementary and complex reactions, molecularity only to elementary reactions

Important Terms:
- Elementary reaction: Reaction that occurs in a single step
- Complex reaction: Reaction occurring in multiple steps
- Rate-determining step: Slowest step in a complex reaction, determines overall reaction rate
